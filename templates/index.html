Conditions We Detect

Early screening for major neurological and developmental disorders

Parkinson's Disease

Neurological

Detects reduced saccade velocity, hypometric saccades, and increased fixation duration.

  • Reduced saccade velocity (<300 deg/s)
  • Hypometric saccades (undershooting)
  • Prolonged fixations (>300 ms)

Alzheimer's Disease

Cognitive

Identifies increased saccade latency, impaired visual search, and prolonged fixations.

  • Prolonged fixations (>350 ms)
  • Reduced visual exploration
  • High saccade variability

Autism Spectrum Disorder

Developmental

Recognizes atypical scan paths, reduced social attention, and unique fixation patterns.

  • High fixation variability (>200 ms std)
  • Atypical spatial attention
  • Elevated saccade velocity

ADHD

Developmental

Detects shortened fixations, elevated saccade rate, and high spatial dispersion.

  • Shortened fixations (<150 ms)
  • Elevated saccade rate (>4/sec)
  • High movement variability
